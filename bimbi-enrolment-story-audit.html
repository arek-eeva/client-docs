2. Online First Impression

Does your website and social presence reflect the real culture and warmth of your centre?

Website quality Fair Your website is professionally structured and well-organised across multiple pages: programmes, philosophy, curriculum, and kindergarten are all covered clearly. The site uses real facility photos rather than stock images. However, text carries most of the weight, and nothing on the homepage communicates the everyday warmth that families are actually looking for.
Video content Fair You have recently produced video content with your agency, including family-ownership history, staff and centre manager interviews, and director-led pieces, all shot without children present. That is the right production approach for the current sector climate. The gap is deployment: at the time of this audit, none of that video is embedded on the homepage or visible in the website navigation. Families researching you online will not find it. The asset exists; the website is not yet doing the work to put it in front of prospective parents.
Social media presence Fair A Facebook page exists and is linked from the website. Post frequency and recency were not confirmed during this audit due to platform access restrictions. No Instagram account was identified. Without regular content on at least one channel, prospective families have no way to follow the life of your centre between interest and enrolment.

Key finding

You have already done the hardest part. The video content is produced, the production approach is compliant with the way the sector is heading, and the stories you chose to tell (family ownership, staff, centre managers) are the right ones. The next step is making that content work on every channel a family touches before they call you. Embedding a director-led piece on the homepage and a short staff-and-family film on your About page would change the first impression a researching parent gets, immediately. Your website currently tells families what your centre does. Putting your existing video to work is what will make them feel it.

3. Enquiry Response

How quickly and effectively does your centre follow up when a family reaches out?

Response speed Fair Enquiries come through a general contact form that requires name, email, and a message. There is no dedicated tour booking pathway, which means families who want to visit need to wait for a manual response. Phone is available during hours, which helps, but there is no after-hours self-service option.
Tour booking process Needs Work No online tour booking tool is visible on the contact page or anywhere on the site. Families who research in the evening or on weekends must wait until the next business day to take action. This delay is where warm leads go cold.
Follow-up process Needs Work There is no visible re-engagement process for families who enquire but do not immediately book. Without an automated follow-up sequence, families who browse and contact multiple centres are likely to choose the one that stays in touch most consistently.

Key finding

The gap between enquiry and enrolment is where most family decisions are won or lost. Right now, a family who fills in your contact form on a Sunday evening will wait until Monday before they hear from you — by which time they may have already booked a tour elsewhere. A dedicated tour booking option, even a simple calendar link, removes that friction entirely. Paired with an automated two or three-touch follow-up sequence, this closes the loop that most independent centres leave open.

Rating on Google listing Fair NQS rating visibility on your Google Business Profile could not be confirmed during this audit. Most families comparing early learning centres have no way to look up ACECQA ratings on their own — they rely on what centres show them. A single line above the fold on your homepage, plus a mention in your Google Business Profile description, is a ten-minute change that builds immediate credibility with every family who finds you online.

A competitive suburb with a clear gap to close

Airport West and its surrounding suburbs are served by a mix of independent operators and national group providers. Group operators have a structural advantage in content consistency — they produce video and run automated follow-up sequences at scale. What they cannot replicate is the warmth and authenticity of a family-owned centre that has been part of a community since 2006. That story is yours alone, and right now it is not being told online. The centres that consistently fill their books in competitive suburbs are not always the ones with the highest ratings. They are the ones whose story is visible, whose content is consistent, and whose enquiry process removes every possible barrier between interest and a booked tour.

Your Three Quick Wins

Three specific things you could do this month to strengthen your enrolment pipeline.

1

Put your existing video to work on the homepage

You have already produced strong video content with your agency. The fastest, highest-return move is to embed the director-led piece on your homepage above the fold, with a shorter family-and-staff cut on your About page. Families researching you online should see your story within seconds of landing, not have to dig for it. No new production required. This is a deployment step that turns work you have already paid for into a working part of your enrolment funnel. If a 30 to 45-second highlight cut does not already exist, that single edit (taken from the longer pieces you have) is the only new asset you need to maximise impact.

2

Add a tour booking link and display your NQS rating above the fold

Two changes, each taking under an hour to implement. First, add a simple online calendar booking link to your contact page and homepage — this removes the single biggest drop-off point in your enquiry process. Families who research in the evening should be able to secure a tour time without waiting for business hours. Second, add a clear line near the top of your homepage: "Rated Meeting NQS by ACECQA." A brief explanation of what this means builds immediate credibility. Most families do not know to look up the ACECQA register — you need to show them.

3

Start a Google review process after every tour and family milestone

Identify one person at the centre responsible for sending a direct Google review link to every family after their first tour. A short, warm message with a one-click link removes all friction. Ten to fifteen new reviews over the next three months would significantly improve your local search visibility and give prospective families the social proof they are looking for when they compare options. Review volume and recency are among the strongest signals Google uses to rank local business listings — this is free, immediate, and compound in its effect.
