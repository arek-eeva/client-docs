Google Search Visibility

Google Business Profile quality, review volume, rating, and owner engagement

Strong

Star Rating

A 5.0 average on Google is a genuine asset. That's a perfect score, and families searching for care in Deer Park will see it immediately. It signals that the families already with you are highly satisfied, and it builds trust at the very first moment a new parent searches for options.

Fair

Review Volume

Twenty-six reviews supports the 5.0 rating well for a single-site independent centre. That said, several competitors in your postcode have gathered 37 to 61 reviews, which can make their listings feel more established to a first-time searcher. Growing your review count steadily — even by five or six more per year — keeps the momentum going and reinforces your standing.

Fair

Owner Responses

This could not be confirmed during the live review. Responding publicly to every review — positive or not — is one of the highest-leverage actions on your listing. It shows prospective families that your team is attentive and values feedback. If responses are not currently consistent, this is a quick habit to build.

Fair

Listing Completeness

Your listing includes the key contact details and maps correctly. To get more from it, consider adding a regular stream of photos directly to your Google profile, keeping your description current, and using the Posts feature to highlight seasonal news, open days, or enrolment availability. An active profile ranks higher and gives families more reasons to click through.

2

Online First Impression

Website quality, visual storytelling, video presence, and social media

Fair

Website Quality

Your site is professionally structured, with a clear menu, a dedicated book-a-tour page, and useful information across programs, philosophy, and curriculum. It functions well as a reference point. What it lacks is warmth — the kind that comes from seeing real children, real educators, and real moments at the centre. The homepage currently leads with icons and brand graphics rather than photography of life at Deer Park, which means a first-time visitor can read about the centre but can't yet feel it.

Fair

Video Presence

You have recently produced a body of video content with your agency, covering family-ownership history, staff and centre manager interviews, and director-led pieces, all filmed without children present. That production approach is well-aligned with where the sector is heading. The gap at the time of this audit is deployment: none of that video is yet embedded on the homepage or visible in the website navigation. The work is done. The website is not yet putting it in front of the families who are researching you online.

Needs Work

Real Photography

The homepage visuals consist primarily of icons and logo elements. There are no photos of actual children, educators, or the centre environment visible on the homepage or key landing pages. A gallery page exists, but families researching you won't typically navigate there before forming an initial impression. Authentic photography — of your space, your meals, your outdoor areas, your educators — needs to be front and centre on the pages families land on first.

Fair

Social Media

A Facebook page exists and is linked from your website. Activity levels and post frequency could not be confirmed during this review. Facebook remains the primary social channel for ECE decision-makers in your market, particularly parents in the 25-40 age group. If posting is infrequent, the page can actually work against you — a family who clicks through and sees a last post from six months ago will wonder whether the centre is still active. Consistent, regular content is the fix.

3

Enquiry Response

Tour booking process, post-submission experience, and re-engagement

Strong

Tour Booking Page

You have a dedicated book-a-tour page — which puts you ahead of many centres that bury enquiry options in a general contact form. The form collects name, phone, email, and preferred tour day and time, which is exactly the information needed to follow up efficiently. This is a solid foundation.

Fair

Post-Submission Experience

After submitting the tour form, families receive a message that staff will be in touch shortly. There is no automated confirmation email, no instant acknowledgement, and no indication of when to expect contact. For a family who may have submitted to two or three centres at once, the first centre to respond — and to make them feel seen — tends to win the tour. A simple automated acknowledgement, sent immediately on form submission, goes a long way.

Needs Work

Re-engagement and Nurture

There is no visible sequence for families who enquire but don't book, or who tour but don't enrol. Once a family submits the form, the entire follow-up relies on manual staff action. There is no nurture email, no "here's what to expect on your tour" message, no follow-up content to keep your centre top of mind during the weeks a family takes to decide. This is the gap between centres that fill their books and those that wonder what happened to that enquiry from last month.

Fair

Contact Accessibility

Phone and email contacts are clear and consistent across the site. The 1300 number is easy to find. The contact page includes a general enquiries form as well as the separate tour booking form, which is a sensible distinction. Adding a direct email address on the contact page — rather than only a form — gives families who prefer email a more direct route.

4

Quality Rating Visibility

NQS/ACECQA rating on website and Google listing

Needs Work

NQS Rating on Website

Your centre has not yet received an ACECQA rating. The website has a dedicated National Quality Framework page, which is a positive signal — it shows families you take quality seriously. However, the page states only that you "aim to exceed the national quality standards" without providing a published rating outcome. Until the assessment is complete, this page is doing partial work. Updating it to explain what the assessment process involves and what families can expect from your approach will make it more meaningful for prospective parents.

Needs Work

NQS on Google Listing

Without a current ACECQA rating, there is nothing to display on your Google Business Profile. This is understandable for a centre still early in the assessment cycle. When your rating is confirmed, adding it to the business description and to your website homepage will be one of the quickest visibility wins available. Families searching for "Exceeding NQS childcare Deer Park" are specifically filtering for quality — you want to be visible to those searches.

Fair

Quality Signals in Place

Even without a formal rating, your website communicates quality in several ways: the NQF page, your school transition program, your meals program, and your philosophy documentation all tell a story of a centre that takes its responsibilities seriously. The gap is that these signals are spread across interior pages and aren't anchored to a headline rating that prospective families can point to. Building content around the assessment journey — and ultimately the result — will close this gap.

The Opportunity

Deer Park sits in a suburb with genuine competition — ten centres within 5 km, several with higher review volumes and at least two holding Exceeding NQS ratings. But the content picture is almost entirely blank across your local market. With only one competitor using video, and most sitting on static websites with little active social presence, there is a clear opening for a centre that commits to telling its story consistently. Your 5.0 Google rating is already doing work for you. The question is whether the full picture — what families see when they land on your website, click through from social, or submit a tour enquiry — matches the promise of that rating. Right now, there is a gap. Closing it, through authentic content and a reliable follow-up system, is what separates centres that fill steadily from those that rely entirely on word of mouth and hope.

Three Quick Wins

01

Deploy your existing video and photography on the homepage

You have already produced strong video content and you have photography on hand from your recent shoot. The highest-return move is to put that work where families actually look first: the homepage above the fold. Embed a 30 to 60-second director-led piece, swap the icon-led hero for two or three real photos of your space and your educators, and add a second video on the About page. No new production required. This turns work you have already invested in into a working part of your enrolment funnel.

02

Set up an instant tour confirmation email

When a family submits your tour booking form, they should receive an automated email within seconds. Thank them for reaching out, confirm what happens next, and give them something to look forward to — a short description of what the tour involves, or a link to your gallery or philosophy. It keeps you front of mind while they wait for a call.

03

Respond to every Google review, starting today

Go back through your 26 reviews and respond to any that don't yet have an owner reply. From this point forward, respond to every new review within 48 hours. A brief, personal response — acknowledging the family, thanking them, and reinforcing one specific thing they mentioned — takes two minutes and is visible to every family who reads your listing. It's one of the most effective signals of a well-run centre.
