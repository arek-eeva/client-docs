1. Google Search Visibility

What a family sees when they search for childcare near Narre Warren, before they ever call you.

Your rating Strong A perfect 5.0 is as good as it gets. Families trust it, and you have earned it.
Review volume Fair 17 reviews is a start, but it's thin next to nearby centres, so you can be scrolled past.
Listing photos Needs work Your listing shows barely one real photo. Families judge a centre on what they can see.

Key finding

Your reputation is excellent where families find it. The problem is how little there is to find. A 5.0 rating with only 17 reviews and almost no photos means a parent comparing three centres has little to hold onto, even though the real experience you offer is strong.

2. Online First Impression

Does your website reflect the real warmth of walking through your door?

Website Needs work A single template page, no enquiry form, and a leftover footer credit from the template maker.
Video Needs work No video anywhere. Families deciding on care want to see the rooms and the educators.
Social presence Fair You have a Facebook page, but no Instagram, where a lot of young Narre Warren parents look first.

Key finding

The centre a family meets online isn't the one they meet in person. Your rooms are warm and your community is real, but the website is a single unfinished page with a template credit still in the footer, and there's no video to carry the feeling across. This is the widest gap in the whole audit, and the one most worth closing.

3. Enquiry Response

What happens when a family decides to reach out.

Ways to enquire Needs work The only option online is a phone call. There's no tour-request form and no email on the site.
Tour booking Needs work Nothing a family can do after hours. A parent researching at 9pm has no way to act.
Follow-up Fair Enrolments run on paper, so there's no automatic reminder if a family goes quiet.

Key finding

Every enquiry depends on a family picking up the phone during your busy day. The parents most likely to fill your empty places are researching in the evening, and right now there's no way for them to raise their hand until morning, by which point they've often booked a tour somewhere else.

4. Quality Rating Visibility

Are you using the rating you have already earned?

Rating on display Needs work Your Meeting NQS rating appears nowhere on your website or your Google listing.

Key finding

You hold a Meeting NQS rating across all seven quality areas, and you show it nowhere. It's a trust signal you've already paid for with hard work, and putting it in front of families costs nothing.

The opportunity

This is a crowded corner of Narre Warren, and on the one number families use to compare, your closest neighbour has almost twice your reviews. The good news is that a rating like yours is rare, so the gap isn't about quality. It's about being seen. The centres that fill quickly here aren't the highest rated, they're the ones a family can actually picture before they visit, through steady, real content rather than a single listing.

Your Three Quick Wins

Three things you could do this month, at no cost, to strengthen your enrolment pipeline.

1

Put your rating where families look

Add your 5.0 star rating and your Meeting NQS rating to your website and your Google listing. You've earned both, and right now a searching family sees neither.

2

Fill your Google listing with real photos

Add a dozen genuine photos of your rooms, your yard and your educators. It's free, it takes an afternoon, and it's the first thing a parent scrolls through when they find you.

3

Give families a way to enquire after hours

Add a simple tour-request form to your site so a parent researching in the evening can raise their hand without waiting to call. Even a basic form captures enquiries you are losing overnight.
