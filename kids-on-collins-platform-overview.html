The Core Idea

Parents decide emotionally, then justify logically. Often only one parent tours. Video creates the emotional trust. The ECE platform puts that trust to work, so fewer families fall through the gaps between enquiry and enrolment.

It's Already Working

Kids on Collins has seen significant improvement in the current reporting period (Dec 2025 to Jan 2026 vs the same period the year before). The EEVA videos are a contributing factor, with families commenting on them during tours.

The ECE platform is the next step: automating the follow-up, tracking, and reporting that currently sits on Jenn's shoulders, so the centre can sustain and build on these results without relying on one person's time and memory.

What Is the ECE Platform?

The Enrolment Content Engine is EEVA's integrated marketing and enrolment management system, built specifically for early learning centres. It runs on GoHighLevel (GHL), a platform EEVA manages on behalf of each centre.

It is not a CRM in the traditional sense. It does not replace QikKids, Storypark, or any operational system the centre already uses. It handles one job: making sure families who enquire are followed up with consistently, professionally, and on time.

What stays the same: QikKids still handles enrolment paperwork, subsidies, and CentreLink. Storypark still handles parent communication for enrolled families. The ECE platform handles everything before enrolment, plus reputation management and reporting.

1
Tour-to-Enrolment Engine
Ensures no enquiry falls through the gaps. Replaces manual follow-up with a structured, automated system.
  • Tour booking calendar that syncs to Jenn's calendar (replacing Calendly, which didn't sync properly)
  • Automated confirmation and reminders sent by email when a family books a tour (24 hours before, with parking, building access, and FAQ)
  • Post-tour nurture sequence of 4 emails designed to help families decide: a thank-you with video for the partner who didn't attend, CCS information, social proof from other families, and a gentle follow-up
  • Pipeline tracking so every family is visible at their current stage: Contacted, Registered for Tour, Toured, EOI Submitted, Enrolled, Orientation Booked, Started
  • Waitlist management with automatic updates when places become available
What this means for Kids on Collins
  • Jenn no longer needs to manually email every family before and after tours
  • The partner who didn't attend the tour gets video content and centre information automatically
  • Tour no-shows (a current pain point) are reduced with timely reminders including building access details
  • Every enquiry is tracked. Nothing gets lost because "time got away"
2
Review and Reputation Engine
Systematically builds the centre's Google review presence and catches issues before they become public complaints.
  • Automated review requests sent to families at the right time (typically 30-60 days after enrolment)
  • Smart routing: happy families are directed to leave a Google review. Families who are neutral or unhappy are routed privately to Jenn for a personal conversation
  • Google Business Profile optimisation with correct categories, professional imagery, and updated information
  • Sentiment tracking so the centre can see trends over time, not just individual reviews
What this means for Kids on Collins
  • Alumni parents (the "gold mine" Jenn mentioned) can be contacted for reviews from their time at the centre
  • Current families imported from Storypark/QikKids can be gently prompted
  • Negative experiences are caught privately, before they become public reviews
  • Review count grows steadily without Jenn having to personally ask each family
3
Google Maps and Local Visibility
Wins the "childcare near me" search. Makes the centre visible, accurate, and appealing when families are browsing online.
  • Google Maps pin correction (currently directing families to the wrong building)
  • Google Business Profile management with professional photos, video, correct hours, and complete listing fields
  • Directory consistency across Google, CareforKids, Starting Blocks, and other listing platforms
  • Conversion tracking so the centre knows where enquiries are actually coming from
What this means for Kids on Collins
  • The Google Maps issue is a critical fix. Families searching "childcare Collins Street" are currently sent next door
  • Once fixed, the listing will be fully optimised with EEVA video content and professional imagery
  • Families will be able to "feel" the centre before they even call
4
Pipeline and Occupancy Dashboard
Gives directors, the foundation team, and the board clear numbers they can act on. No more guessing.
  • Live dashboard showing enquiry count, tour booking rate, conversion rate, pipeline breakdown, and review metrics
  • Year-over-year comparison matching the format of the current Optimise marketing reports
  • Custom date ranges for ad-hoc reporting
  • Printable board report for formal governance meetings
Built around Ashlee's 10 board questions
  • The dashboard is specifically designed to answer the questions the board needs answered
  • Carly and Ashlee can log in anytime to see live data, no need to wait for monthly reports
  • Russell and Jessica will have appropriate access to view reporting as needed

Social Media Management

Optimise Marketing (Chris) continues to handle website and social media content for Kids on Collins. As part of the ECE platform setup, Optimise will move onto the GHL social media planner.

  • Post scheduling and approval: Optimise queues content, Jenn approves before it goes live
  • Analytics visible: reach, engagement, top-performing posts all available in the dashboard
  • Restricted access: Optimise can access the social planner and ads section only. No access to contacts, automations, or other platform features
  • LinkedIn: handled separately by consultant Natasha Davey-Lisle (not through GHL)

Data and Privacy
