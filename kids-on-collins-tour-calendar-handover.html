The short version

Your part is the website: repoint the tour buttons to the ECE calendar and retire the Calendly link. Everything behind the booking is already built on our side. Lanes stay as they are, you own the site and the buttons, EEVA owns the calendar and what happens after a booking.

Recommended best practice Worth doing

Repointing the buttons is the whole job. These are the things that lift how many of those clicks turn into a booked tour, in rough priority order.

1. Use one consistent label everywhere

Right now it's a mix of "Book a Tour" and "BOOK A TOUR". Pick one wording and one case (we'd suggest Book a Tour in title case) and use it on every tour button. Consistency makes the action instantly recognisable as families move through the site.

2. Make the tour button the standout action

On any page where "Book a Tour" sits next to "Enquire" or "Contact Us", the tour button should be the visually dominant one (the bold, filled, accent-colour button) and the enquiry options secondary. Booking a tour is the action most likely to lead to an enrolment, so it should win the eye.

3. Add a persistent tour button on mobile

Most KoC enquiry traffic is on a phone. A small sticky "Book a Tour" bar pinned to the bottom of the screen on mobile keeps the action one tap away no matter how far someone scrolls. Keep the tap target a comfortable size (around 44px tall).

4. Repeat the CTA at the end of long pages

The program and room pages (Infants, Toddlers, 3yo and 4yo Kinder) currently lean on the header and footer for the tour button. Add a "Book a Tour" call to action at the bottom of each of those pages, so a parent who's just finished reading about the room they want can book without scrolling back up.

5. Add a line of reassurance next to the button

A short line under or beside the button lowers hesitation, for example "Book a 20-minute visit, pick a time that suits you." Small reassurance like duration and "choose your own time" measurably reduces drop-off at the click.

6. Keep the click behaviour consistent

Decide whether the booking page opens in the same tab or a new tab and apply it to every tour button. We'd suggest the same tab so the browser back button works naturally, but either is fine as long as it's uniform.

7. Optional — embed the calendar inline

If you'd like families to book without leaving the site at all, use the embed code on a dedicated "Book a Tour" page (linked from the main nav) instead of linking out. Fewer clicks to a visible calendar usually means more completed bookings. Linking out is perfectly fine too — this is a nice-to-have, your call with Arek.

8. Track the booking as a conversion

In GA4 / Tag Manager, treat a click on the tour buttons (or the booking page load) as a conversion event, the same way the Calendly click was tracked, so we can see which pages are driving tours. If you want UTM tags on the inbound links, send Arek your naming convention and he'll make sure they carry through.

Retire the Calendly link

Once the ECE buttons are live and tested, nothing on the site should still point to calendly.com/kids-on-collins. A quick site search for "calendly" will confirm it's all gone. The Calendly account itself can be left dormant or closed, just make sure no live button links to it. If any ad landing pages or social bio links use the old Calendly URL, repoint those to the ECE booking URL too so paid and social traffic lands in the right place.
