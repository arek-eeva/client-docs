Our approach

Children can do real things. We prepare the room, then get out of the way.

Little Fern follows Montessori practice without treating it as scripture. The rooms are prepared so children can manage real life at their own scale: pour their own water, cut their own fruit, sweep their own crumbs, hang their own coat on a hook they can reach.

We keep the environment low in stimulation on purpose. Natural light, timber, linen, clay and paper. No screens, no character merchandise, no background music running all day. When a room is quiet and ordered, children settle into long stretches of concentration, and concentration is where learning happens.

Our director walks through the five areas of the Montessori room and why nothing in it is there by accident.

The prepared environment

Every shelf holds a purpose and everything has a place. Materials are real: glass jugs, ceramic bowls, brooms that work. Children move confidently through a room they can predict.

Unhurried time

The morning work cycle runs for two protected hours. Deep concentration needs a long runway, so nobody is herded from one activity to the next to fill a timetable.

Nature, daily

The garden is open in all weather. Children plant, water, harvest, compost and get properly muddy. Gumboots live at the door and spare clothes live in every locker.

Honest partnership with parents

You will know your child's educators by name and they will know you. Our documentation is specific about what your child worked on, not a highlights reel.

Programs

Three rooms, one continuous approach.

Children move up when they are ready, not when the calendar says so. Each room is named for a fern that grows in our garden.

Maidenhair

Nursery, 6 weeks to 2 years

  • 12 places across two small groups
  • One educator to three children, above the required one to four
  • A dedicated sleep room with cots and floor beds

Babies need one steady person, so each child in Maidenhair has a primary carer. The same educator settles your baby, learns their cues, does their bottles and nappies, and writes to you at the end of each day. Sleep follows your child's own rhythm, never a roster, and you can ring at any point of the day to hear how things are going.

Bracken

Toddlers, 2 to 3 years

  • 15 places
  • One educator to four children
  • Direct access to the toddler garden and mud kitchen

Toddlers want to do it themselves, so we let them. Dressing, pouring, wiping tables, carrying trays and climbing the mound are the curriculum. Language runs through everything: songs, stories, and the real names of real things. Toileting is unhurried and led by each child's readiness, worked out together with you.

Tree Fern

Preschool and kinder, 3 to 5 years

  • 22 places
  • One educator to eight children, above the required one to eleven
  • Government funded three and four year old kindergarten

Tree Fern runs a funded kindergarten program led by Daniel Okafor, a Victorian Institute of Teaching registered teacher, within the full Montessori day. Children work with number rods and sandpaper letters, cook, sew, do woodwork with real tools, and carry responsibilities like the compost roster and setting lunch tables. By the final year most children read their own name in the sign-in book and pack their own bag for school transition visits.

Free Kinder applies at Little Fern, so a funded kindergarten program costs families nothing for 15 hours a week within your enrolled days.

A day here

The rhythm is the same every day. That is the point.

Young children relax when they know what comes next. Our day keeps the same shape all year, and the detail changes with the seasons and the children's interests.

  1. 7.30Doors open, slow arrivals

    Breakfast is on until 8.30, porridge or toast, and there is always a quiet lap available if the morning at home was rushed.

  2. 8.45Morning work cycle

    Two protected hours. Children choose their work from the shelves: practical life, language, number, art, care of the room. Educators observe more than they interrupt.

  3. 11.00Garden, every day

    In every weather. Digging, watering the vegetable beds, checking the worm farm, climbing the mound, watching the builders next door when there are builders next door.

  4. 12.15Lunch, cooked on site

    Rosa cooks a seasonal menu in our kitchen each morning. Children set the tables, serve themselves and scrape their own plates. The menu is posted in the foyer each Monday.

  5. 1.00Rest

    Beds with linen sheets, curtains drawn, a quiet room. Non-sleepers look at books or do quiet work after twenty minutes. Nobody is made to lie still for two hours.

  6. 2.30Afternoon studio

    Clay, drawing, weaving and woodwork at the kinder end. Smaller groups and longer projects, some of which run for weeks.

  7. 4.30Late afternoon, unwinding

    Back to the garden or the big room for open play. A relaxed afternoon tea, then stories until pick up. Doors close at 6.

Our educators

Fourteen adults for 49 children, and they stay.

Staff turnover is the quiet problem in early childhood. Ours is low because the conditions are good: above-award pay, planning time off the floor, and rooms staffed above required ratios so nobody is stretched.

  • Qualified, and supported to keep studying. Every educator holds a Diploma or Bachelor qualification or is completing one with paid study leave.
  • Continuity of carer. Your child keeps the same primary carer within each room, and moves up with a familiar face alongside.
  • Cleared and current. Everyone on site holds a Working with Children Check, first aid, anaphylaxis and asthma training, renewed on schedule.

Fees and the Child Care Subsidy

We charge one daily fee that covers everything above, published in our enrolment pack and reviewed once a year. Most families receive the Child Care Subsidy, which we claim on your behalf, so you pay only the gap between the subsidy and the fee.

What that gap is depends on your family income and activity hours, so a headline fee tells you little on its own. Bring your details to a tour and we will estimate your actual weekly cost on the spot, before you join the waitlist.

Free Kinder covers 15 hours a week of the three and four year old kindergarten program, and we apply it directly to your account.
