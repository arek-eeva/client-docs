1Arrival day — Santa Monica, Mexican breakfast, hat shop, The Garland

2Hollywood day — pivot, then Griffith at sunset
Tue 31 March

The day started with a failed Griffith attempt — the car park was already full by 12:07 — so we pivoted instead of forcing it. That sent us into Hollywood Boulevard for Hard Rock Cafe lunch, souvenirs, and Ripley’s Believe It or Not, which Mayan had an absolute blast in. Later we gave Griffith another shot around 5:00 pm, parking was suddenly easy, and the day ended properly with the observatory, city views, authentic Mexican food from a food truck, and back to The Garland.

3Universal Studios Hollywood
Wed 1 April

Universal turned into a big, full-bodied park day. We started in Harry Potter, though Mayan was just a touch too small for Hogwarts Adventure, then kept moving through Secret Life of Pets, Scooby-Doo, Minions, lunch at Hogs Head, Kung Fu Panda, Simpsons and the giant doughnut, Studio Tour, the Lower Lot, Jurassic area and Dino Play, Super Mario, Transformers, then the shuttle back. 4LA to San Diego
Thu 2 April

This was a strong transition day rather than a dead travel day. We checked out at 11:00, drove Mulholland Drive, landed in Sherman Oaks for breakfast, came over the hill through Beverly Hills, then did the Tar Pits and LACMA area before heading south. A quick charge and ice cream at Solana Beach broke up the run nicely, and the day finished at 10864 Lamentin Ct in Tierrasanta with Stan and Pam, pizza, whisky, and a long catch-up.

5SeaWorld day
Fri 3 April

SeaWorld sounds like it belonged to Mayan from the start. The cleaner fish — the “sucky fish” — became such a favourite that we went back to them three separate times. Around that we did the tower, the sea lion show, the whale show from the splash zone like maniacs and got properly soaked, then lunch, the Arctic exhibit, and churros.

9Balboa Park day
Tue 7 April

Balboa Park turned into a very solid day. We started at the Comic-Con Museum for the Doctor Who exhibition, and Mayan did a scavenger hunt there. Then we walked across, had ice cream, and ended up in the Fleet Science Center, where we saw a beautiful otter film in the IMAX theatre — Mayan’s first IMAX experience. On the way out we spent some time at an awesome playground in Balboa Park. After that we headed back, and the day finished with a really fun Japanese Shogun Kobe dinner where they cooked in front of us.

10LEGOLAND
Wed 8 April

LEGOLAND was a good day, but also a crowded one. There was a 30+ minute queue of cars just waiting to get into the car park, and once inside the park was busy enough that we did not end up doing that many rides. Even so, we got the dino boat ride, the dad-and-son pull-up tower, the Galaxy Coaster, the water park, and the two palaces that were actually open. One of the nicest parts was the NINJAGO ninja training playground, where Mayan met a nice little boy and they played together. Mayan also got a pair of swords to wear ninja-style on his back, and then Kate and Mayan did the BIONICLE Blaster ride, which was basically spinning cups, while I wisely stayed off it because it looked too nauseating.

11Coronado + Seaport Village day
Thu 9 April

Thursday started with driving over the Coronado Bridge to Coronado Island. We spent a couple of hours around Ferry Landing Park, hired a two-row bike, and cruised along the coast. After ice cream and some souvenir shopping, we drove through downtown and ended up at Seaport Village, where we wandered through the shops, saw the novelty sock store and other fun little places, had proper burritos, and picked up a MAGA hat, another wide-brim wool hat, and authentic dream catchers. In the end Mayan decided not to go on the historic carousel. Back at Stan and Pam’s, I smoked dry-brined rib-eye steaks and we enjoyed them with three beautiful wines, including a 2008 Heretic they had kept for us and the Barking Mad we brought from Australia.

12Santee Lakes + Stone Brewery day
Fri 10 April

We went to Santee Lakes and hired a purple dragon paddle boat. It was a hot, sweaty ride, but Mayan liked it. Beer-wise, I tried Arrogant Bastard, then a flight of Stone Dayfest IPA, Yuzu You, Mocha IPA, and Triple IPA, and followed that with a pint of Double Delicious IPA.

13San Diego to Anaheim
Sat 11 April

We packed in the morning, had the last breakfast with Grandpa Stan and Grandma Pam — as Mayan calls them — and hit the road at noon. We stopped for Chick-fil-A on the way north, then broke the drive at Newport Beach for ice cream, some beach adventure time, and a playground stop. After charging up the Tesla, we made it to the hotel across the road from Disneyland Park, and by evening we were wandering between the parks having a look and considering staying for the fireworks. The day ended with a late dinner at Denny’s.

14Disneyland day
Sun 12 April

We got to Disneyland right as it opened at 8:00 am after a night of rain, with a bit of drizzle still hanging around for the first few hours. We started at the Robinson Crusoe Treehouse, then did Big Thunder Mountain Railroad, which Mayan did not enjoy despite it being a relatively mild roller coaster. After that we went to Pirates of the Caribbean — Mayan’s favourite so far — and then the Haunted Mansion, which suited him well because he likes spooky things. After Winnie the Pooh we got ponchos because the rain was unrelenting. Then, while waiting for Indiana Jones Adventure, the ride temporarily closed with technical difficulties, but we kept waiting because at least we were out of the rain. After that Kate went to get a coffee while Mayan and I went back through the treehouse two more times, then together we did the Enchanted Tiki Room before heading towards Tomorrowland. There we did Star Tours in 3D with R2-D2 and C-3PO, had pizza, used our Lightning Lane to get onto Space Mountain, and then did the Buzz Lightyear blaster ride.
