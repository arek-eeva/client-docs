Day-by-day Los Angeles plan

This section is the working version: what to do, why it fits the day, where to eat, and the direct links you’re most likely to need while out and about.

1Arrival in LA + Santa Monica reset day
Mon 30 March
LAXSanta Monica PierCountry General StoreThe Garland

Plan: Land at LAX, clear immigration, collect the Tesla around 7:00 am, head straight to Santa Monica, have lunch, and do something beach-adjacent so the day properly starts in California rather than in transit mode. After that, either go to The Garland first to unpack and reset, then head out to Country General Store for the hat mission and dinner, or go directly to the hat shop if energy is still strong. The real goal is to extend the day and stay awake until about 8:00 pm so the timezone change lands cleanly.

Why this works with a six-year-old: Santa Monica gives you an easy, high-reward first block: ocean, pier, movement, novelty, room to wander. Then the second half of the day stays flexible. You can choose hotel-first if everyone needs a reset, or keep rolling if the energy is still there. That is much better than forcing a rigid zig-zag across LA on arrival day.

  • Land, immigration, bags, Tesla pickup
  • Drive to Santa Monica
  • Lunch near the beach
  • Santa Monica Pier, short beach walk, Tongva Park, or another nearby low-friction stop
  • Then choose: hotel first to unpack, or Country General Store first if momentum is good
  • Hat stop — Country General Store, 6279 Van Nuys Blvd, Van Nuys
  • Dinner and keep the family moving gently until about 8:00 pm
Eat: Anchor lunch near Santa Monica rather than trying to improvise too much. Best fit is Mel’s Drive-In Santa Monica for diner vibes, burgers, fries and shakes. For an easy Mexican option that still works with a six-year-old, Tacos Por Favor is a very solid call. If you do hotel first and then head to the hat shop later, dinner can happen after Country General Store rather than trying to make Santa Monica carry the whole day.

2Hollywood / Griffith full day
Tue 31 March
Travel TownGriffithTCLMel’s

Plan: Start around 10:00 am and make this a proper Hollywood-area day without overcomplicating it: Travel Town first if Mayan is in the mood, Griffith once it opens at noon, then a controlled Hollywood Boulevard hit, lunch, one optional extra, and back to The Garland before everyone gets wrecked.

Why this works with a six-year-old: It uses a good rhythm rather than forcing one long slog. Travel Town is easy kid mileage, Griffith gives the views and science curiosity, and Hollywood works best as a short burst of movie-tourist nonsense rather than a marathon. The whole shape leaves room for jet-lag weirdness without wasting the day.

  • 10:00 am — leave The Garland
  • 10:20–11:45 — Travel Town Museum (easy first stop)
  • 12:15–1:30 — Griffith Observatory and sign views
  • 2:00–3:00 — Hollywood Walk of Fame + TCL Chinese Theatre
  • 3:00–4:00 — lunch in Hollywood
  • 4:00–5:30 — one optional extra only: Academy Museum of Motion Pictures, Petersen, or straight back to hotel
  • 5:30 onward — Garland reset, pool/rest, simple dinner
Eat: Mel’s Drive-In Hollywood is the best lunch fit today if you want classic American diner energy without too much faffing around. Shake Shack Hollywood is the safest efficient option if everyone is cooked. If dinner still happens out and you want one fun LA move, Pink’s Hot Dogs is the obvious chaotic tourist option — otherwise keep dinner simple near the hotel.
Mel’s HollywoodShake ShackPink’sAcademy Museum
Opening-time logic
Travel Town opens at 10:00 am. Griffith Observatory opens at 12 noon, so do not plan Griffith as the first stop. Hollywood Boulevard is flexible, but it is better as a midday stop than an absurdly early start. Petersen remains a valid optional swap for today if you decide you want an indoor museum instead.

3Universal Studios Hollywood
Wed 1 April
Universal StudiosNintendo infoPark map

Plan: Full park day. Use The Garland shuttle. Arrive before opening and prioritise Nintendo first.

Why this works with a six-year-old: Universal is best when the day is built around excitement and stamina, not completionism. Super Nintendo World brings the big wow factor early, and because Mayan is a big Harry Potter fan, Wizarding World should be treated as a genuine priority rather than an optional afterthought. Studio Tour gives everyone a breather, and the rest of the day can flex depending on mood and energy.

  • Super Nintendo World first
  • Mario Kart while waits are still reasonable
  • Studio Tour late morning
  • Lunch inside the park
  • Wizarding World of Harry Potter as a real priority
  • Minions / Kung Fu Panda based on energy
  • Shuttle back before everyone gets feral
Eat: Keep it convenience-first but still fun. Krusty Burger / Springfield is the best fit for lunch because it is playful, themed and easy with a kid. Toadstool Cafe is great if timing works, but not worth turning into a headache. If you still want a treat later, Voodoo Doughnut or Johnny Rockets at CityWalk are good holiday-energy options.

4Check out, Tar Pits, Farmers Market, drive to San Diego
Thu 2 April
Tar Pits ticketsTar Pits mapFarmers Market

Plan: Turn the transfer day into one last useful LA stop. Tar Pits makes far more sense here than cramming it into another day.

Why this works with a six-year-old: La Brea is one of those rare museum-style attractions that already feels weird and exciting to a child — bubbling tar, mammoths, fossils, giant extinct animals. But if you decide you would rather get moving sooner, this day should also offer southbound options that are easy to reach and easy to cut short.

  • Check out of The Garland
  • Choose either a central LA stop or a southbound freeway-friendly stop
  • Lunch before the longer run to San Diego
  • Drive south to Tierrasanta
  • Settle in and keep the evening easy
Eat: If you want the easiest central LA version, Original Farmers Market is still the most flexible lunch move. If you’d rather get properly on the road and stop further south, look at King Taco in Long Beach / south LA orbit for an authentic casual Mexican option, or an easy coastal lunch around San Clemente before the final run to San Diego.

Departure day

Mon 13 April · Anaheim to LAX

Plan: This should work as a low-pressure final California day rather than dead airport time. Check out in the morning, have one deliberately chosen westside stop that helps Mayan burn energy, then head to the airport corridor early and without stress.

  • 10:00 AM — check out of Grand Legacy
  • 10:00 AM to 11:15 AM — easy drive north, with coffee / snack / comfort stop if needed
  • 11:30 AM to 1:30 PM — choose a proper westside stop
  • 1:30 PM to 2:30 PM — lunch nearby
  • 2:30 PM to 4:30 PM — final low-stress activity or beach / playground time
  • By around 5:00 PM — head toward the airport zone
  • Before 8:00 PM — return the Tesla
Best departure-day option
Dockweiler Beach is the strongest fit: close to LAX, easy to understand, plenty of space for Mayan to run around, and emotionally a nice last California stop. You can keep it brief or stretch it depending on energy.
Other realistic options
Manhattan Beach Pier if you want a nicer town feel and a walkable lunch area, or Huntington Beach Pier if you want to make more of the day and don’t mind the extra commitment. I would avoid anything more ambitious than that.
Eat: Keep lunch easy and coastal. Around Manhattan Beach or Dockweiler / Playa del Rey, just aim for burgers, tacos or fish-and-chips level simplicity. For an authentic but still approachable Mexican option on this final day, a casual taco stop near the westside is a better idea than trying to do a big sit-down meal.
